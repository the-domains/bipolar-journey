My tale of the ebb and flow of Manic Depressive Syndrome

The Beginning

I was born June 8th, 1969 in Salem Oregon, with some unusual events.. My Mother was put to sleep, only never to see me again for 25 years as I would be adopted by two other people, one whom I considered my Dad, father and one who had the role of Mother but due to her own mental illness never new how to cope in life.

No, this isn’t me, close, but I don’t have pictures of myself when I was real young, just fleeting glimpses of pictures I remember seeing of a platinum blonde blue eyed child… So, I did the next best thing to warm your hearts and found one that looked almost as cute as I did here.

35 And back

Here is really the starting of my bipolar from a medical standpoint, not from a biological standpoint. I was diagnosed having BP (bipolar) after having a complete meltdown from “God Syndrome”. For those of you who aren’t BP, there is a huge rush in being able to do everything, knowing you can do anything, try anything and be anything…… Until you can’t, don’t and then you lose touch with reality, your loved ones and you crash and crash hard. This is why I finally went to the doctor.

After going on multiple meds, being diagnosed, I started to do a retrospective of my life and found that since a child I had BP, since a teenager I had full onslaught of BP and had been a total risk taker… and since an adult BP had been with me, but had tried to live a normal life.

I found my wife (it was love at first sight) when I was 21, however we did the opposite of normal couples; she got pregnant we got married and figured things out as we went along. I think my BP played a part in staying married as the struggles were many, but here we are 25 almost 26 years later and still married. I am the most blessed man especially for her to stick around me and what I go through (spending sprees, etc) that are part of BP.

So as you can see BP can have its perks (if you don’t let it overtake you and send you to jail) and it has its drawbacks of deep depression and agony.
